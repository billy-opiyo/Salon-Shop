# Royal Braids / Salon Shop — Ad Video Script With Voice-Over

This document is a ready-to-use production script for creating an online showcase/ad video for the Royal Braids salon web platform. It covers the **main customer website** and the **admin console**, with suggested visuals, text overlays, and complete voice-over narration.

---

## 1. Video Goal

Create a polished promotional video that shows Royal Braids as more than a salon website: it is a complete digital salon platform for customers, staff, admins, bookings, content, communication, and security.

### Target Audience

- Potential clients looking for salon services online.
- Salon owners or business partners who want to see the system capabilities.
- Recruiters, portfolio viewers, or online audiences reviewing the web project.
- Social media viewers on LinkedIn, Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, or X.

### Recommended Style

- Tone: premium, confident, modern, smooth.
- Music: elegant Afrobeat, soft luxury background, or cinematic corporate music.
- Voice: warm, professional, clear, energetic.
- Visual pace: fast enough for an ad, slow enough to understand the product.

---

## 2. Recommended Video Versions

### Full Showcase Version

- **Length:** 3 to 5 minutes.
- **Best for:** YouTube, LinkedIn, portfolio presentation, project demo page.
- **Purpose:** Show the main public website and admin console in one complete overview.

### Short Social Version

- **Length:** 45 to 60 seconds.
- **Best for:** Instagram Reels, TikTok, YouTube Shorts, WhatsApp status.
- **Purpose:** Quick attention-grabbing project highlight.

---

## 3. Recording Checklist

Before recording, prepare the website so the video looks clean and professional.

- Open `public/index.html` for the main website.
- Open `public/admin.html` for the admin console.
- Use a clean browser window with bookmarks hidden if possible.
- Use demo data for bookings, gallery styles, reviews, messages, and admin stats.
- Avoid showing private real emails, phone numbers, API keys, or customer details.
- Record in 1920×1080 or 1440×900 for a clean desktop ad.
- Also capture a few mobile-size clips to show responsiveness.
- Use smooth zooms on important UI areas such as buttons, counters, tabs, and dashboard cards.

## 4. Full Showcase Video Script — Scene by Scene

### Scene 1 — Opening Hook: Premium Salon Experience

**Time:** 0:00–0:12  
**Visuals:** Show the Royal Braids splash screen, loading progress bar, luxury background image, and animated Royal Braids title reveal.  
**Text Overlay:** `Royal Braids — A Complete Digital Salon Platform`

**Voice-Over:**

> Welcome to Royal Braids — a premium salon experience brought online through a complete, modern web platform designed for beauty, bookings, client care, and business management.

---

### Scene 2 — Homepage Hero and Brand Introduction

**Time:** 0:12–0:28  
**Visuals:** Scroll from the hero section. Show the navigation menu, rotating logo, “Book Now” button, login button, and salon hero image. Toggle dark/light mode briefly if it looks good.  
**Text Overlay:** `Elegant design. Smooth navigation. Instant booking access.`

**Voice-Over:**

> The main website welcomes clients with elegant salon branding, smooth navigation, a responsive layout, dark mode support, and clear calls to action that guide visitors straight to services, gallery, booking, reviews, blog, and contact.

---

### Scene 3 — Service Categories

**Time:** 0:28–0:48  
**Visuals:** Scroll to the Services section. Click through service category tabs: Braids, Hair, Beauty Spa, Nails, Makeup, Barber, Massage & Wellness, Eyebrows & Lashes, Bridal/Event Packages, and All Services. Show service cards with price, duration, and “Book This Service”.  
**Text Overlay:** `Braids • Hair • Spa • Nails • Makeup • Barber • Wellness • Bridal`

**Voice-Over:**

> Clients can explore a full service catalog, from braids and hair styling to spa treatments, nails, makeup, barber services, wellness, lashes, and bridal packages. Each service includes clear details like description, price, duration, and a quick booking action.

---

### Scene 4 — Dynamic Gallery and Style Discovery

**Time:** 0:48–1:12  
**Visuals:** Show Gallery section. Use filters, sorting controls, featured rails such as Trending Braids and Most Booked Braids. Open a before/after image or lightbox if demo images are available. Show favorite/save style action if authenticated.  
**Text Overlay:** `Realtime gallery. Filters. Before & after. Favorite styles.`

**Voice-Over:**

> The gallery helps customers discover their next look. Styles can be filtered by category, braid length, size, and style type. The platform supports trending styles, most-booked looks, before-and-after images, lightbox previews, and favorite styles for signed-in clients.

---

### Scene 5 — Online Booking Flow

**Time:** 1:12–1:42  
**Visuals:** Scroll to Booking. Show fields for name, email, phone, service, date, time slot, stylist, inspiration upload, and special requests. Select a service and time slot. Show success message or booking confirmation flow if safe to demo.  
**Text Overlay:** `Book appointments online with realtime slot availability.`

**Voice-Over:**

> With online booking, customers can choose a service, select a date and available time slot, add a preferred stylist, upload inspiration photos, and submit special requests. Behind the scenes, realtime slot locking helps prevent double bookings and keeps scheduling reliable.

---

### Scene 6 — Waitlist and Booking Automation

**Time:** 1:42–1:58  
**Visuals:** Show waitlist prompt or mention it using a caption. If demo state exists, show waitlist badge/queue chip. Show booking confirmation message and client prompt to log in.  
**Text Overlay:** `Waitlist support • Email confirmations • WhatsApp reminders`

**Voice-Over:**

> If a selected slot becomes unavailable, the system can offer a waitlist option. Booking workflows are designed for automation, including confirmation emails, WhatsApp notifications, reminders, and waitlist updates when appointment slots open.

---

### Scene 7 — Client Authentication and Dashboard

**Time:** 1:58–2:20  
**Visuals:** Open login modal, show sign-in/register/forgot password options. Then show the authenticated client dashboard if demo login is available. Highlight appointments, reviews, favorites, profile, security/privacy, login history, reschedule/cancel options.  
**Text Overlay:** `Client accounts. Dashboard. Favorites. Appointment self-service.`

**Voice-Over:**

> Clients can create accounts, log in securely, continue as guests, and access a personal dashboard. From there, they can track appointments, manage reviews, save favorite styles, update profile settings, review login history, and use appointment self-service options like rescheduling or cancellation.

---

### Scene 8 — Reviews, Blog, and Contact

**Time:** 2:20–2:40  
**Visuals:** Scroll through Reviews, Blog, Visit Us, and Contact sections. Show review sorting, approved testimonials, blog cards, contact form, location/contact details.  
**Text Overlay:** `Reviews • Blog content • Contact messages • Location details`

**Voice-Over:**

> The public website also includes customer reviews, moderated testimonials, salon blog content, contact forms, visit information, and real-time content updates powered by Firebase.

---

### Scene 9 — Transition to Admin Console

**Time:** 2:40–2:52  
**Visuals:** Switch to `admin.html`. Show the Admin Console heading, Back to Homepage button, login form, password visibility toggle, and admin-only access message.  
**Text Overlay:** `Private Admin Console — Operations in one place`

**Voice-Over:**

> Behind the customer experience is a powerful private admin console where salon staff can manage operations, content, bookings, communication, access, and security from one organized dashboard.

---

### Scene 10 — Admin Access and Permissions

**Time:** 2:52–3:08  
**Visuals:** Show admin login success if possible. Highlight tab row. Show tabs: Bookings, Waitlist, Schedule, Gallery, Blogs, Reviews, Messages, Services, Admins, Security.  
**Text Overlay:** `Role-based access • Permission-scoped admin tabs`

**Voice-Over:**

> Admin access is protected with Firebase Authentication and permission-based records. Depending on role and access level, admins only see the sections they are allowed to manage, including bookings, content, security, and admin delegation.

---

### Scene 11 — Booking Management

**Time:** 3:08–3:32  
**Visuals:** Show Bookings tab. Highlight total/pending/confirmed/waitlisted/completed/cancelled counters. Click filters. Show booking cards with status actions such as Confirm, Move to Confirmed, Complete + Release Slot, or Cancel + Release Slot.  
**Text Overlay:** `Realtime bookings. Status filters. Lifecycle-safe actions.`

**Voice-Over:**

> The Bookings tab gives staff realtime visibility into every appointment. Admins can filter by status, review client details, inspect special requests and inspiration images, then safely confirm, complete, cancel, or release booking slots through protected backend workflows.

---

### Scene 12 — Waitlist and Schedule Board

**Time:** 3:32–3:55  
**Visuals:** Switch to Waitlist then Schedule. Show queue details, waitlisted bookings, day/week schedule views, date navigation, event cards, status legend, and selected booking details.  
**Text Overlay:** `Waitlist queue • Day/week schedule • Calendar-style planning`

**Voice-Over:**

> The waitlist tools help staff manage customers waiting for unavailable slots, while the schedule board organizes bookings into day and week views. Staff can navigate dates, inspect appointment details, and take quick workflow actions directly from the schedule.

---

### Scene 13 — Gallery and Blog Content Management

**Time:** 3:55–4:18  
**Visuals:** Show Gallery tab and Blogs tab. Highlight create/edit/delete forms, category fields, image upload, before/after image support, featured flags, live preview, publish checklist, and blog post fields.  
**Text Overlay:** `Manage gallery styles and blog content in realtime.`

**Voice-Over:**

> Content management is built directly into the console. Admins can create and update gallery styles, upload images through Cloudinary, add before-and-after visuals, mark featured looks, and publish blog posts that appear on the public website in realtime.

---

### Scene 14 — Reviews and Messages

**Time:** 4:18–4:42  
**Visuals:** Show Reviews tab with counters and moderation actions. Show Messages tab with status filters, sorting, and message cards.  
**Text Overlay:** `Review moderation • Contact inbox • Customer communication`

**Voice-Over:**

> The admin console also supports review moderation and client communication. Staff can approve, reject, edit, reply to, feature, or delete reviews, while contact messages are organized by status, sorting controls, and realtime inbox updates.

---

### Scene 15 — Service Visibility Controls

**Time:** 4:42–4:58  
**Visuals:** Show Services tab. Toggle categories on/off if safe, show active and inactive groups, counters, save button. Then briefly cut back to public Services/Booking dropdown if possible.  
**Text Overlay:** `Control public service categories live.`

**Voice-Over:**

> Service categories can be switched on or off from the admin console. These settings update the public services section, booking dropdown, and gallery filters, giving the salon full control over what customers can see and book.

---

### Scene 16 — Admin Delegation

**Time:** 4:58–5:12  
**Visuals:** Show Admins tab. Highlight role, active/inactive state, permissions for bookings, content, security, and admin management.  
**Text Overlay:** `Super-admin delegation with permission controls`

**Voice-Over:**

> Super admins can delegate access by creating and updating admin users, assigning roles, activating or deactivating accounts, and controlling permissions for bookings, content, security, and admin management.

---

### Scene 17 — Security Monitoring and Incident Response

**Time:** 5:12–5:45  
**Visuals:** Show Security tab. Highlight login activity, risk filters, security counters, online sessions, security alerts, account change history, activity timeline, CSV/Excel export, and response actions if visible.  
**Text Overlay:** `Login monitoring • Risk scoring • Sessions • Alerts • Account actions`

**Voice-Over:**

> Security is a major part of the system. Admins can monitor login activity, failed attempts, suspicious behavior, online sessions, account changes, alerts, and user timelines. For incident response, authorized admins can temporarily block accounts, force logout, require password resets, or clear restrictions through backend-controlled actions.

---

### Scene 18 — Technology and Backend Power

**Time:** 5:45–6:05  
**Visuals:** Use animated text or quick clips of Firebase/Firestore/Cloud Functions concepts, then show public site and admin side by side.  
**Text Overlay:** `Firebase • Firestore • Auth • Cloud Functions • Cloudinary • Email • WhatsApp`

**Voice-Over:**

> The platform is powered by Firebase Authentication, Cloud Firestore realtime data, Firebase Hosting, Cloud Functions, Cloudinary media uploads, email confirmations, WhatsApp automation, and secure Firestore rules.

---

### Scene 19 — Closing Call to Action

**Time:** 6:05–6:25  
**Visuals:** Return to homepage hero, gallery, booking CTA, then final brand/logo screen.  
**Text Overlay:** `Royal Braids — Beauty meets smart digital operations.`

**Voice-Over:**

> Royal Braids is not just a salon website. It is a complete digital salon solution — built for beautiful client experiences, efficient staff workflows, realtime operations, secure management, and business growth. Book, manage, connect, and grow — all in one platform.
